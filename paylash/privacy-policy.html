Summary

paylash lets you split bills on your own and — if you subscribe to premium — share groups and expenses with friends across iPhone, iPad, Mac, and the web. This page explains exactly what we collect, why, and what control you have.

The short version: the bill splitter and itemized split work entirely on your device and require no account. Everything else (groups, events, shared expenses) needs an account, and the data you create there is stored in Google Firebase so your devices and your group members can see it. We never sell your data. We never run ads that profile you.

Who is responsible

paylash is built and operated by NAE Dev. For privacy questions, write to nae_dev@icloud.com. We are the data controller for the personal data described below.

If you only use the bill splitter

The Expense tab and the Itemized tab (the bill splitter, OCR receipt scan, per-person split) work without signing in. All input — amounts, people counts, line items, the receipt photo if you attach one — stays on your device. We don't transmit or store any of it.

For free-tier users on the Expense and Itemized tabs, paylash also shows a non-personalized banner ad. See "Advertising" below.

If you create an account and use Groups

The Groups tab requires a paid premium subscription. When you subscribe and sign up, we process the following personal data:

  • Email address — used as your account identifier, for sign-in, for email verification, and for password reset. We send no marketing email.
  • Password — handled by Firebase Authentication. We never see your password; it is hashed and stored by Google on our behalf.
  • Profile — an optional nickname, first name, last name, and an avatar (emoji or initials). You choose what to provide. The nickname is visible to other members of any group you join; the names and avatar are visible inside the app on your devices.
  • Preferred language — the language you pick in Settings, mirrored to your account so notifications arrive in your language.
  • Premium subscription state — whether your subscription is active and when it expires. This is mirrored from Apple's StoreKit so other devices you sign in to can see your premium status without re-checking the App Store.
  • Push notification tokens — issued by Apple for each device you've enabled notifications on. We use them to deliver notifications about your groups.
  • Groups, events, expenses, settlements you create — your user-generated content, including any group photo an admin sets. Stored in Firebase Firestore, visible to other members of the same group or event.
  • Activity metadata — timestamps and the user ID of who last updated a group, event, or expense, so the app can show "you" vs "another member" attribution.

How we use your data

Each category above has a single, specific purpose tied directly to the feature it powers — we don't reuse data for unrelated purposes. We do not build advertising profiles. We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for their own marketing.

Our legal bases for processing (under EU/UK GDPR):

  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) — account creation, sign-in, syncing your groups across your devices, delivering notifications you've opted into, processing the premium subscription.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) — keeping the service running and secure (e.g., rate-limiting abuse, cleaning up unverified accounts), debugging crashes, basic analytics through Firebase that don't profile individuals.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) — non-personalized advertising on free-tier surfaces, where consent is required by EU/UK/Swiss law or by certain US state laws. You can change or withdraw consent at any time.

What stays only on your device

  • Your appearance preference (System / Light / Dark)
  • Your default currency
  • Your in-progress bill in the Expense and Itemized tabs (so you can return mid-edit)
  • Any receipt photo you attach (uploaded to the group's expense only when you save; not before)

Who else processes your data

We rely on a small set of trusted infrastructure providers. Each has its own privacy policy linked below.

  • Google Firebase (Google LLC) — Authentication, Firestore database, Cloud Functions, and Cloud Messaging for push delivery. Your account and group data is stored in Google's EU data center in Belgium (europe-west1), and all of our serverless functions also run in europe-west1. Firebase Authentication and Cloud Messaging use Google's globally-distributed infrastructure. Firebase Privacy.
  • Apple — handles your in-app purchase as the merchant of record. Apple processes the subscription, holds the payment record, and sends us only an entitlement signal indicating whether your account is currently premium. Apple also delivers our push notifications (APNs). Apple Privacy.
  • Google AdMob — serves a single banner ad on the free-tier Expense and Itemized tabs. We request non-personalized ads only — no advertising identifier (IDFA), no cross-app tracking, no advertising profile of you. AdMob receives basic non-identifying signals (approximate region from IP, ad-interaction events, device model and OS version) used purely to deliver and measure the contextual ad. Google Privacy.
  • Frankfurter (frankfurter.app) — used only when you display amounts in a currency different from the expense's source currency. We send the currency codes (e.g., "EUR,TRY") and never any identifier, account data, or amount.

International data transfers

Your account and group data is stored on Google Cloud servers in Belgium (europe-west1, European Union), and all of paylash's serverless processing happens in the same region. If you are in the EU/UK/Switzerland, your data stays in the EU — no international transfer occurs.

If you are outside the EU (United States, Turkey, Latin America, or any other non-EU region), creating an account transfers your data to the EU for storage and processing. We rely on Google's standard contractual safeguards for those transfers.

Your rights

If you are in the EU/UK/Switzerland, GDPR gives you the following rights. Equivalent rights exist for residents of California (CCPA/CPRA), Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, and Virginia. We honor all of them regardless of where you live.

  • Access — see what we have. Most of it is visible in the app itself; for a machine-readable export, email us.
  • Rectification — fix it. Edit your profile and your expenses through the app. For account email changes, contact support.
  • Erasure — delete it. Go to Settings → Profile → Delete Account. Your account and all owned groups, events, and expenses are removed. Expenses you contributed to a group still owned by other members may be retained without your identifying details, since other members rely on the financial history.
  • Portability — get a copy. Email nae_dev@icloud.com and we'll provide a JSON export within 30 days.
  • Restriction & objection — request that we pause specific processing or object to it. Email support.
  • Withdraw consent — for AdMob ads in regulated regions, open Settings → Manage privacy choices. Withdrawal does not affect processing before the withdrawal.
  • Complaint — you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

Data retention

We keep your data while your account is active. Unverified accounts created with no follow-up are automatically deleted after 7 days. When you delete your account, we remove your profile and the groups you own. Expenses you contributed to groups owned by other people may be retained — they're part of the group's financial history — but your identifying details are stripped where feasible. Backup snapshots of Firestore may persist briefly after deletion under Google's standard retention.

Children's privacy

paylash is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 13. The App Store rating reflects the app's intended audience. If you believe we've inadvertently collected data from a child, email us and we'll remove it.

App Tracking Transparency

paylash does not show the iOS App Tracking Transparency prompt because we do not track you across apps or websites. Our advertising SDK is configured for non-personalized ads only, which removes the need to request tracking permission.

Advertising

Free-tier users see one banner ad at the top of the Expense and Itemized tabs. Premium subscribers see no ads anywhere. The banner is always non-personalized.

If you launch paylash from the EU, UK, Switzerland, or one of the regulated US privacy states (California, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, Virginia), Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) shows a one-time consent form on first launch. You can change or revoke your choice at any time from Settings → Manage privacy choices. Outside those regions, no consent form is shown and the Settings row is hidden.

Security

All traffic between paylash and our servers uses TLS encryption. Passwords are hashed by Firebase Authentication using industry-standard methods (we never see your password). Firestore enforces server-side rules so a user can only read and write their own data and the groups they belong to. We log security-relevant events for abuse detection; these logs are retained for a maximum of 30 days.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy we'll update the "Last updated" date at the top. Material changes will be communicated via the App Store listing or in-app notice on the next release.

Contact
Privacy questions, data export requests, complaints — email nae_dev@icloud.com. We respond within 30 days, usually within a week.

Zusammenfassung

Mit paylash kannst du Rechnungen für dich allein teilen und — wenn du Premium abonnierst — Gruppen und Ausgaben mit Freunden auf iPhone, iPad, Mac und im Web teilen. Diese Seite erklärt genau, was wir sammeln, warum, und welche Kontrolle du hast.

Die Kurzfassung: Der Rechnungsteiler und die postenweise Aufteilung funktionieren vollständig auf deinem Gerät und benötigen kein Konto. Alles andere (Gruppen, Ereignisse, geteilte Ausgaben) benötigt ein Konto, und die dort erstellten Daten werden in Google Firebase gespeichert, damit deine Geräte und deine Gruppenmitglieder sie sehen können. Wir verkaufen deine Daten niemals. Wir schalten niemals Werbung, die dich profiliert.

Wer ist verantwortlich

paylash wird von NAE Dev entwickelt und betrieben. Bei Datenschutzfragen schreib an nae_dev@icloud.com. Wir sind der Datenverantwortliche für die unten beschriebenen personenbezogenen Daten.

Wenn du nur den Rechnungsteiler nutzt

Der Ausgaben-Tab und der Postenweise-Tab (Rechnungsteiler, OCR-Belegscan, Pro-Person-Aufteilung) funktionieren ohne Anmeldung. Alle Eingaben — Beträge, Personenanzahl, Posten, das Beleg-Foto, falls du eins anhängst — bleiben auf deinem Gerät. Wir übertragen oder speichern davon nichts.

Für Nutzer ohne Premium zeigt paylash im Ausgaben- und Postenweise-Tab zusätzlich ein nicht-personalisiertes Banner an. Siehe „Werbung" unten.

Wenn du ein Konto erstellst und Gruppen nutzt

Der Gruppen-Tab erfordert ein kostenpflichtiges Premium-Abonnement. Wenn du abonnierst und dich registrierst, verarbeiten wir die folgenden personenbezogenen Daten:

  • E-Mail-Adresse — wird als Konto-Kennung, für die Anmeldung, zur E-Mail-Verifizierung und zum Passwort-Zurücksetzen verwendet. Wir senden keine Marketing-E-Mails.
  • Passwort — wird von Firebase Authentication verwaltet. Wir sehen dein Passwort nie; es wird gehasht und in unserem Auftrag von Google gespeichert.
  • Profil — ein optionaler Spitzname, Vorname, Nachname und ein Avatar (Emoji oder Initialen). Du entscheidest, was du angibst. Der Spitzname ist für andere Mitglieder der Gruppen, denen du beitrittst, sichtbar; die Namen und der Avatar sind in der App auf deinen Geräten sichtbar.
  • Bevorzugte Sprache — die in den Einstellungen gewählte Sprache, die mit deinem Konto synchronisiert wird, damit Benachrichtigungen in deiner Sprache eintreffen.
  • Premium-Abonnementstatus — ob dein Abonnement aktiv ist und wann es ausläuft. Dies wird aus Apples StoreKit gespiegelt, damit andere Geräte, auf denen du dich anmeldest, deinen Premium-Status sehen können, ohne den App Store erneut abzufragen.
  • Push-Benachrichtigungs-Tokens — von Apple für jedes Gerät ausgegeben, auf dem du Benachrichtigungen aktiviert hast. Wir verwenden sie, um Benachrichtigungen über deine Gruppen zuzustellen.
  • Gruppen, Ereignisse, Ausgaben, Abrechnungen, die du erstellst — deine nutzergenerierten Inhalte, einschließlich eines von einem Admin festgelegten Gruppenfotos. In Firebase Firestore gespeichert, für andere Mitglieder derselben Gruppe oder desselben Ereignisses sichtbar.
  • Aktivitäts-Metadaten — Zeitstempel und die Benutzer-ID, wer eine Gruppe, ein Ereignis oder eine Ausgabe zuletzt aktualisiert hat, damit die App „du" gegenüber „ein anderes Mitglied" anzeigen kann.
